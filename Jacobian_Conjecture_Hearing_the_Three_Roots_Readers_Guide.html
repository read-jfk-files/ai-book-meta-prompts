Hearing the Three Roots A Reader’s Guide to the Proposed Classical Geometry Behind the 2026 Jacobian Counterexample

A cubic is a point of projective three-space. Mark one of its roots and a natural three-sheeted map appears. Remove the finite folds and the remaining map is locally invertible. The decisive question is not where the collision comes from—it comes for free— but whether the carved-out source is really ordinary affine three-space.

1 2 3
Narrative spine. Root selection supplies the global collision for free; all of the difficulty is relocated into making the spaces around that collision look like ordinary affine space.
Part 0 · Orientation

The story, the symbols, and the audit

Backslash deletes; slash usually identifies.

There are two stories here, and a trustworthy guide must keep them separate. The first is an exact polynomial certificate: a displayed map \(F:\mathbb C^3\to\mathbb C^3\) has constant Jacobian determinant \(-2\) and sends three different inputs to one output. That alone defeats the Jacobian Conjecture in dimension \(3\). The second is a geometric story proposed to explain why such a map should exist: cubics form \(\mathbb P^3\), choosing one root gives three sheets, repeated roots create folds, and strategic deletion may leave an étale three-to-one map between two copies of affine space.

Verified certificate Let \(u=1+xy\) and \[ \begin{aligned} P&=u^3z+y^2u(4+3xy),\\ Q&=y+3xu^2z+3xy^2(4+3xy),\\ R&=2x-3x^2y-x^3z. \end{aligned} \] Direct symbolic differentiation gives \(\det J_F=-2\), while \[ F\!\left(0,0,-\frac14\right)= F\!\left(1,-\frac32,\frac{13}{2}\right)= F\!\left(-1,\frac32,\frac{13}{2}\right)= \left(-\frac14,0,0\right). \] The determinant identity and the collision are finite algebraic calculations.
Proposed interpretation The symmetric-product construction is a compelling classical skeleton for the phenomenon. Its target is visibly \(\mathbb A^3\). Its source is not visibly \(\mathbb A^3\). Unless an explicit isomorphism or a fully applicable recognition theorem is supplied, the source identification remains the load-bearing step rather than routine bookkeeping.

0.1 The construction in ordinary English

  1. A binary cubic is determined by four coefficients up to common scale, hence by a point of \(\mathbb P^3\).
  2. Its three roots form an unordered triple of points of \(\mathbb P^1\).
  3. If one root is marked, a generic cubic has three possible markings.
  4. When roots repeat, some markings fuse. This is ramification.
  5. Delete the fused markings upstairs, and the remaining map has no finite critical points.
  6. Delete one hyperplane downstairs, obtaining \(\mathbb A^3\).
  7. Delete its preimage upstairs. If that open threefold is also \(\mathbb A^3\), one obtains an étale, globally noninjective polynomial self-map of \(\mathbb A^3\).

0.2 Deletion is not quotienting

Deletion

\[ X\setminus Z=\{x\in X:x\notin Z\}. \] Points are removed. Nothing is glued to anything else.

Quotient

\[ X/{\sim}. \] Different points are declared equivalent and replaced by an equivalence class.

The construction uses both operations. The symmetric product \(\operatorname{Sym}^3(\mathbb P^1)=(\mathbb P^1)^3/S_3\) forgets ordering by quotienting. Every backslash in the carved-open spaces means deletion.

Misconception “Isomorphic” does not mean merely “the same number of points,” “homeomorphic,” “diffeomorphic,” or “birational.” An algebraic isomorphism must preserve regular functions. Affine-space recognition is difficult precisely because many exotic varieties look ordinary to topology while carrying a different algebra of functions.
Checkpoint 0 · Put the chart identity into words

Translate \(\mathbb P^1\setminus\{\infty\}\cong\mathbb A^1\).

Solution. Remove one projective point, say \([1:0]\). On the remaining chart \(v\neq0\), every point has a unique representative \([t:1]\), so the coordinate \(t=u/v\) identifies the chart with the affine line.

Part I · Spaces made of polynomials

1 · Affine space, projective space, and infinity

Projective space is affine space with all escape directions recorded.

1.1 Affine space remembers polynomial functions

Complex affine \(n\)-space is \(\mathbb A^n_{\mathbb C}=\mathbb C^n\), but algebraic geometry remembers more than the underlying set or topology. Its regular functions form the polynomial ring \[ \mathcal O(\mathbb A^n)=\mathbb C[x_1,\ldots,x_n]. \] A map is algebraic when its coordinate functions are polynomial. An isomorphism must have an algebraic inverse—not merely a continuous or smooth inverse.

1.2 Projective space records directions

Projective \(n\)-space is \[ \mathbb P^n=(\mathbb C^{n+1}\setminus\{0\})/\mathbb C^\times. \] A point is written \([x_0:\cdots:x_n]\), with \[ [x_0:\cdots:x_n]=[\lambda x_0:\cdots:\lambda x_n],\qquad \lambda\neq0. \] Each chart \(x_i\neq0\) is an affine \(n\)-space. The extra projective points are not mystical; they encode directions in which affine coordinates can diverge.

Key idea The pathology of an étale noninjective polynomial map cannot occur through a finite fold. It must be visible in a compactification: some preimage runs toward the projective boundary while its image stays finite.

1.3 Hyperplane complements

Theorem · elementary chart theorem If \(H\subset\mathbb P^n\) is a hyperplane, then \[ \mathbb P^n\setminus H\cong\mathbb A^n. \]

What it says. Removing one projective hyperplane opens a standard affine chart.

Why it matters. The target-side identification in the proposed construction is immediate.

Proof sketch. After a projective change of coordinates, take \(H=\{x_0=0\}\). Every point outside \(H\) has \(x_0\neq0\) and a unique representative \([1:t_1:\cdots:t_n]\). The coordinates \(t_i=x_i/x_0\) give the isomorphism.

1.4 Why compactify first

In affine geometry, parallel lines appear not to meet and roots can “disappear” as coefficients degenerate. In projective geometry, intersection counts remain stable when multiplicity and points at infinity are included. The compactification is a ledger for escape.

Checkpoint 1 · Verify a projective chart

Show that \(\mathbb P^2\setminus\{X=0\}\cong\mathbb A^2\).

Solution. Every point has \(X\neq0\), so rescale to \([1:Y/X:Z/X]\). The inverse sends \((s,t)\) to \([1:s:t]\).

2 · A cubic is a point

The coefficients are coordinates; the roots are anatomy.

2.1 Binary cubics

A binary cubic is \[ f(X,Y)=AX^3+BX^2Y+CXY^2+DY^3. \] Multiplying all coefficients by a nonzero scalar does not alter its projective zero set. Thus a nonzero cubic determines a point \[ [A:B:C:D]\in\mathbb P^3. \] This is the first load-bearing translation: a polynomial has become a geometric point.

2.2 Roots live on \(\mathbb P^1\)

A nonzero linear form \(\ell=uX+vY\) vanishes at one projective point. Over \(\mathbb C\), every cubic factors as \[ f=\ell_1\ell_2\ell_3, \] so it determines an unordered triple of points of \(\mathbb P^1\), with repeated factors represented by multiplicity.

Definition · symmetric product \[ \operatorname{Sym}^n(X)=X^n/S_n. \] Its points are unordered \(n\)-tuples \(\{p_1,\ldots,p_n\}\), with repetitions allowed. Algebraic geometers also write the corresponding effective divisor \(p_1+\cdots+p_n\).
Classical theorem · roots ↔ coefficients \[ \operatorname{Sym}^n(\mathbb P^1)\cong\mathbb P^n. \]

What it says. Unordered degree-\(n\) root data and homogeneous polynomial coefficients are the same parameter space.

Why it matters. \(\operatorname{Sym}^3(\mathbb P^1)\) is exactly the projective space of binary cubics.

Proof sketch. Multiply the \(n\) linear forms. Their coefficients are symmetric functions of the roots, so they survive the quotient by \(S_n\). Conversely, factorization over \(\mathbb C\) recovers the multiset of roots.

2.3 Mark one root

Define \[ \pi:\mathbb P^1\times\operatorname{Sym}^2(\mathbb P^1) \longrightarrow\operatorname{Sym}^3(\mathbb P^1) \] by \[ \pi(p,\{q,r\})=\{p,q,r\}. \] The source remembers a cubic together with a designated root. The target forgets the designation. A cubic with distinct roots \(\{p,q,r\}\) has three preimages: \[ (p,\{q,r\}),\qquad(q,\{p,r\}),\qquad(r,\{p,q\}). \] The generic degree is therefore \(3\).

Checkpoint 2 · Fibers over repeated roots

List the set-theoretic preimages over \(\{1,1,2\}\).

Solution. They are \((1,\{1,2\})\) and \((2,\{1,1\})\). The first is ramified because the designated root \(1\) also appears in the residual pair. The second is unramified: the marked root \(2\) is distinct from both residual roots.

3 · The twisted cubic and the order of kissing

Tangency is a double root; osculation is a triple root.

3.1 Triple roots trace a curve

The small diagonal consists of cubics whose three roots coincide: \[ \Delta_{\mathrm{small}}=\{\{p,p,p\}:p\in\mathbb P^1\}. \] If \(p\) corresponds to the linear form \(uX+vY\), then \[ (uX+vY)^3=u^3X^3+3u^2vX^2Y+3uv^2XY^2+v^3Y^3. \] In coefficient coordinates, \[ \nu_3([u:v])=[u^3:3u^2v:3uv^2:v^3]. \] This degree-\(3\) Veronese curve in \(\mathbb P^3\) is the twisted cubic.

Notation warning Many books absorb the binomial coefficients and write \([u^3:u^2v:uv^2:v^3]\). The two parametrizations are projectively equivalent, but formulas for hyperplanes and discriminants must use one coefficient convention consistently.

3.2 A hyperplane becomes a cubic equation on the curve

A hyperplane \(L(A,B,C,D)=0\) intersects the twisted cubic where \[ L(u^3,3u^2v,3uv^2,v^3)=0. \] The right side is a homogeneous cubic in \(u,v\). Therefore the intersection divisor has total degree \(3\), counted with multiplicity: \[ p+q+r,\qquad 2p+q,\qquad 3p. \]

3.3 Tangent versus osculating

The tangent line at \(p\) remembers first-order motion along the curve. The osculating plane remembers first- and second-order motion. Since a hyperplane in \(\mathbb P^3\) is itself a plane, it can contain the tangent line without equaling the osculating plane.

Generic

Three simple intersection points: \(p+q+r\).

Tangent

One double point and one simple point: \(2p+q\).

Osculating

One triple point: \(3p\).

The exact tangent-but-not-osculating model used below is \[ H=\{B=0\}. \] Along the twisted cubic, \(B=3u^2v\), so the intersection is \(2[0:1]+[1:0]\). By contrast, \(D=0\) pulls back to \(v^3=0\) and is osculating.

3.4 The tangent developable is the discriminant

A repeated-root cubic has the form \(\ell^2m\). Fixing \(\ell\) and varying \(m\) traces the tangent line at \(\ell^3\). Sweeping over all \(\ell\) produces the tangent developable of the twisted cubic. Thus one classical surface has two names:

Classical identification The quartic surface of binary cubics with a repeated root is the tangent developable of the twisted cubic. Its singular locus is the twisted cubic of triple-root cubics.

What it says. Repeated roots are literally tangency in coefficient space.

Why it matters. The branch locus of root-marking has a concrete classical geometry.

Part II · Maps and their folds

4 · Fibers, degree, and the upstairs metaphor

A fiber is the complete answer to “what maps here?”

4.1 Fibers

For \(f:X\to Y\), the fiber over \(y\in Y\) is \[ f^{-1}(y)=\{x\in X:f(x)=y\}. \] Geometers draw the source upstairs and the target downstairs, then imagine all points above the same target point stacked vertically. The metaphor is only a drawing convention, but it is a powerful one.

4.2 Generic degree

A dominant map is generically finite of degree \(d\) if a typical target point has \(d\) preimages. “Generic” means outside a proper lower-dimensional exceptional locus. For root-marking, a cubic with three distinct roots has exactly three markings, so \(\deg\pi=3\).

4.3 A space can be fibered in different ways

The map \((x,y)\mapsto x\) fibers \(\mathbb A^2\) into parallel affine lines. The map \((x,y)\mapsto xy\) has hyperbola fibers for nonzero values and a reducible cross at zero. Fibering is a lens chosen by a map, not an intrinsic label permanently attached to a space.

Checkpoint 4 · Compare two fiberings

Describe the fibers of \((x,y)\mapsto x\) and \((x,y)\mapsto xy\).

Solution. The first has fibers \(\{x=c\}\cong\mathbb A^1\). The second has \(xy=c\): for \(c\neq0\), the fiber is \(\mathbb G_m\); for \(c=0\), it is the union of the two axes.

5 · Ramification and the discriminant

Ramification is where sheets fuse upstairs; the discriminant is the shadow downstairs.

5.1 The root-marking map in coordinates

Represent the designated root by \([u:v]\in\mathbb P^1\) and the residual quadratic by \([a:b:c]\in\mathbb P^2\): \[ \ell=uX+vY,\qquad q=aX^2+bXY+cY^2. \] Multiplication gives \[ \ell q=uaX^3+(ub+va)X^2Y+(uc+vb)XY^2+vcY^3. \] Hence \[ \pi([u:v],[a:b:c])=[ua:ub+va:uc+vb:vc]. \]

5.2 The ramification equation

The root of \(uX+vY\) is \([v:-u]\). It is also a root of the quadratic exactly when \[ q(v,-u)=av^2-buv+cu^2=0. \] Therefore \[ R_\pi=\{av^2-buv+cu^2=0\}. \] The equation is quadratic in \((u,v)\) and linear in \((a,b,c)\), so its divisor class in \(\operatorname{Pic}(\mathbb P^1\times\mathbb P^2)\cong\mathbb Z^2\) is \((2,1)\).

Do not conflate two collisions If the two residual roots collide while the designated root stays different, the cubic lies on the discriminant downstairs, but that source point need not lie in the ramification divisor of root-marking. Ramification asks whether the marked root collides with a residual root.

5.3 The discriminant downstairs

For \[ f(t)=At^3+Bt^2+Ct+D, \] the discriminant is \[ \Delta= B^2C^2-4AC^3-4B^3D-27A^2D^2+18ABCD. \] It vanishes exactly when the cubic has a repeated root. This quartic equation defines the branch surface in \(\mathbb P^3\).

5.4 What deletion does to fibers

Remove \(R_\pi\) from the source. Over a double-root cubic \(\{p,p,q\}\), the ramified marking \((p,\{p,q\})\) disappears, while the unramified marking \((q,\{p,p\})\) can remain. Over a triple-root cubic, every marking is ramified, so the remaining fiber is empty.

Key idea After deleting ramification, the restricted map has no finite fold, yet its number of finite preimages can vary because deleted points and boundary points are no longer part of the space.

6 · Properness and the escape to infinity

If sheets cannot collide, they can disappear only through the boundary.

6.1 Local invertibility does not settle global behavior

A nonzero Jacobian determinant invokes the inverse function theorem at every finite point. Nearby, the map has an inverse. Globally, however, distant points may share an image, and preimages may run away to infinity.

6.2 Proper local homeomorphisms are coverings

Topological rigidity principle A proper local homeomorphism is a covering map under standard local compactness hypotheses. A connected covering of a simply connected space has one sheet.

What it says. Properness prevents sheets from appearing or vanishing.

Why it matters. Since \(\mathbb C^n\) is simply connected, a connected proper étale map to affine space would be an isomorphism.

6.3 Properness as “no bounded output from escaping input”

A map is proper if compact sets have compact preimages. The most useful mental test is sequential: inputs should not leave every compact set while their outputs remain bounded.

The toy map \[ g(x,y)=(x,xy) \] is not proper, because \[ g\!\left(\frac1n,n\right)=\left(\frac1n,1\right)\longrightarrow(0,1) \] while \((1/n,n)\) escapes to infinity.

Failure mode Forbidden by étaleness: two finite sheets merge, forcing a critical point.
Allowed by nonproperness: a finite preimage moves out through infinity while the image converges.

6.4 Where the old intuition broke

The intuition behind the Jacobian Conjecture was locally sound: no finite fold is possible. The mistake was assuming that polynomiality made nonproper escape impossible. The counterexample demonstrates that affine infinity has enough structure to hide a missing sheet.

7 · Étale maps and the unit lemma

Étale means locally algebraically invisible; infinity is where global trouble can hide.

7.1 Étale in the smooth equal-dimensional case

For a morphism between smooth varieties of the same dimension, invertibility of the differential is the local criterion for étaleness. For a polynomial self-map \(F\) of affine space, \[ F\text{ étale}\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \det J_F\text{ vanishes nowhere}. \] Étale maps have no infinitesimal folds or branches at finite points.

7.2 The unit lemma

Unit lemma The only units of \(\mathbb C[x_1,\ldots,x_n]\) are nonzero constants. Consequently, a nowhere-vanishing polynomial on \(\mathbb A^n_{\mathbb C}\) is constant.

What it says. Polynomial functions on affine space cannot vary forever while avoiding zero.

Why it matters. Any étale polynomial self-map automatically has constant nonzero Jacobian determinant.

Proof sketch. If \(fg=1\) in a polynomial ring, then \(\deg f+\deg g=0\), so both have degree \(0\). Equivalently, a nonconstant polynomial generates a proper ideal and has a zero on some algebraic extension by the Nullstellensatz framework.

7.3 The post-counterexample formulation

The former Jacobian Conjecture asserted that every étale polynomial self-map of affine \(n\)-space in characteristic zero is an automorphism. The explicit three-dimensional certificate makes the statement false for every \(n\ge3\) by adjoining identity coordinates. The plane case remains a separate open problem.

Checkpoint 7 · Why the unit lemma fails on a punctured line

Explain why \(x\) is a unit on \(\mathbb A^1\setminus\{0\}\).

Solution. Its inverse \(1/x\) is regular on the punctured line. The coordinate ring is \(\mathbb C[x,x^{-1}]\), whose units include \(cx^m\). Deletion changes the ring of regular functions.

Part III · The proposed carving

8 · Choosing the hyperplane

The correct cut creates one exceptional fiber without breaking the wrong boundary.

8.1 The target is easy

Choose the tangent-but-not-osculating hyperplane \[ H=\{B=0\}\subset\mathbb P^3. \] Then \[ Y=\mathbb P^3\setminus H\cong\mathbb A^3. \] No recognition problem occurs downstairs.

8.2 Pull the hyperplane upstairs

Since the \(B\)-coefficient of \(\ell q\) is \(ub+va\), \[ \pi^{-1}(H)=\{ub+va=0\}. \] Its divisor class is \((1,1)\). Define \[ X=(\mathbb P^1\times\mathbb P^2)\setminus \left( \{av^2-buv+cu^2=0\}\cup\{ub+va=0\} \right). \]

8.3 Fiberwise picture

Fix \([u:v]\in\mathbb P^1\). Both deleted equations are linear in \([a:b:c]\), so each cuts a line in the \(\mathbb P^2\) fiber. Generically the lines are distinct. At one tangency parameter they coincide. That single coincidence changes the fiber complement from \(\mathbb G_m\times\mathbb A^1\) to \(\mathbb A^2\).

8.4 The complete-intersection form of the recognition problem

Let \[ h=av+bu,\qquad r=av^2-buv+cu^2. \] Their bidegrees are \((1,1)\) and \((2,1)\). The class matrix is unimodular: \[ \det\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\2&1\end{pmatrix}=-1. \] In Cox coordinates, the two torus scalings can therefore be normalized by \(h=1\) and \(r=1\). The geometric recognition question sharpens to the explicit ring question \[ \mathbb C[u,v,a,b,c]/ (av+bu-1,\ av^2-buv+cu^2-1) \stackrel{?}{\cong} \mathbb C[s,t,w]. \]

The one glyph \[ X\cong\mathbb A^3. \] Everything before this point constructs a natural three-sheeted map and removes its finite ramification. The target identification \(Y\cong\mathbb A^3\) is a routine hyperplane chart. The source identification is not routine. If explicit mutually inverse algebraic coordinates establish it, the skeleton produces an étale noninjective polynomial self-map of affine three-space. If not, the skeleton remains a persuasive model rather than a completed derivation. Euler characteristic, point counts, homology, or a trivial Picard group cannot substitute for these coordinates.
Audit rule Never accept a one-way rational parametrization as an affine isomorphism without checking denominators and producing a regular inverse. A genuine certificate consists of mutually inverse regular maps or an applicable recognition theorem whose hypotheses are verified line by line.

9 · Euler characteristic as an accounting system

Euler characteristic does not mutate; we compare different spaces.

9.1 Compactly supported Euler characteristic

For complex algebraic varieties, \(\chi_c\) is additive under deletion and multiplicative in suitably stratified fibrations: \[ \chi_c(X)=\chi_c(X\setminus Z)+\chi_c(Z). \] Useful values are \[ \chi_c(\mathbb A^n)=1,\qquad \chi_c(\mathbb P^n)=n+1,\qquad \chi_c(\mathbb G_m)=0. \]

9.2 Two lines versus one

For distinct projective lines \(L_1,L_2\subset\mathbb P^2\), \[ \mathbb P^2\setminus(L_1\cup L_2)\cong\mathbb G_m\times\mathbb A^1, \] hence \(\chi_c=0\). If the two lines coincide, \[ \mathbb P^2\setminus L\cong\mathbb A^2, \] hence \(\chi_c=1\).

9.3 The fine-tuning calculation

Over all but one point of the base \(\mathbb P^1\), the fiber has \(\chi_c=0\). At the one tangency point, the fiber has \(\chi_c=1\). Therefore \[ \chi_c(X)=1. \] This is necessary for \(X\cong\mathbb A^3\), but far from sufficient.

10 · Divisors, units, homology, and the invariant audit

Matching invariants means only that the suspect has passed another test.

10.1 Divisor classes

The Picard group of \(\mathbb P^1\times\mathbb P^2\) is \[ \operatorname{Pic}(\mathbb P^1\times\mathbb P^2)\cong\mathbb ZH_1\oplus\mathbb ZH_2. \] The deleted divisors have classes \[ [R_\pi]=(2,1),\qquad [\pi^{-1}(H)]=(1,1). \] Their determinant is \(1\), so they generate the whole Picard group. The divisor exact sequence then suggests \(\operatorname{Pic}(X)=0\) and no nonconstant units—both consistent with \(\mathbb A^3\).

10.2 Why invariant matching cannot certify identity

An invariant is designed to remain unchanged under isomorphism. Therefore different invariant values disprove an isomorphism. Equal values do not reverse the implication. Two nonisomorphic spaces may share many invariants.

Part IV · Recognizing affine space

11 · Affine space is easy to write down and hard to recognize

Looking like affine space is not the same as having affine coordinates.

11.1 The surface warning from Ramanujam

Ramanujam’s characterization · 1971 A smooth complex affine surface that is contractible and simply connected at infinity is isomorphic to \(\mathbb A^2_{\mathbb C}\).

What it says. Ordinary topology plus controlled topology at infinity can recognize the affine plane.

Why it matters. “At infinity” is not an optional afterthought; it is part of the recognition data.

Contractibility alone is not enough. Ramanujam also constructed a smooth contractible affine surface not isomorphic to \(\mathbb A^2\). Its obstruction lies in the topology at infinity.

11.2 The Russell cubic threefold

The Russell cubic \[ \mathcal R=\{x+x^2y+z^2+t^3=0\}\subset\mathbb A^4 \] is smooth and contractible, yet not algebraically isomorphic to \(\mathbb A^3\). The decisive fingerprint is the Makar-Limanov invariant \[ \operatorname{ML}(X)=\bigcap_D\ker D, \] where \(D\) ranges over locally nilpotent derivations.

For affine three-space, \[ \operatorname{ML}(\mathbb A^3)=\mathbb C, \] while for the Russell cubic, \[ \operatorname{ML}(\mathcal R)=\mathbb C[x]. \] Every algebraic additive flow on the Russell cubic must preserve \(x\); affine space has far more translation directions.

11.3 Cancellation

Zariski cancellation asks whether \[ X\times\mathbb A^1\cong\mathbb A^{n+1} \quad\Longrightarrow\quad X\cong\mathbb A^n. \] The answer is negative in positive characteristic in dimensions at least \(3\), through work of Asanuma and Gupta. The characteristic-zero three-dimensional affine-space case remains central and unresolved.

11.4 Coordinate hypersurfaces

The Abhyankar–Sathaye problem asks, roughly, whether a hypersurface whose quotient coordinate ring is a polynomial ring must come from a coordinate after an ambient automorphism. It belongs to the same family of questions: when does an algebraic presentation merely disguise standard affine space?

Open frontier In dimensions at least \(3\), smoothness, contractibility, trivial divisor theory, and familiar point counts may all coexist with exotic algebraic structure. The universally convincing certificate remains explicit affine coordinates with an explicit regular inverse.

11.5 What would settle the source claim

Decisive

  • An explicit ring isomorphism with \(\mathbb C[s,t,w]\).
  • Mutually inverse regular maps \(X\rightleftarrows\mathbb A^3\).
  • A recognition theorem whose hypotheses are checked exactly.

Not decisive

  • \(\chi_c(X)=1\).
  • \(\#X(\mathbb F_q)=q^3\).
  • Trivial Picard group or homology.
  • Contractibility or birationality.
  • A rational parametrization with unchecked denominators.
Part V · The explicit counterexample

12 · From a global idea to a finite certificate

Discovery may be global and conceptual; verification can collapse to two identities.

12.1 The map

Put \(u=1+xy\). Define \(F=(P,Q,R)\) by \[ \begin{aligned} P&=u^3z+y^2u(4+3xy),\\ Q&=y+3xu^2z+3xy^2(4+3xy),\\ R&=2x-3x^2y-x^3z. \end{aligned} \] The component degrees are \((7,6,4)\).

12.2 The determinant identity

Form \[ J_F= \begin{pmatrix} P_x&P_y&P_z\\ Q_x&Q_y&Q_z\\ R_x&R_y&R_z \end{pmatrix}. \] Exact symbolic expansion and simplification gives \[ \det J_F\equiv -2. \] Sampling can build confidence; only the polynomial identity proves constancy.

12.3 The collision identity

Exact substitution gives \[ F\!\left(0,0,-\frac14\right)= F\!\left(1,-\frac32,\frac{13}{2}\right)= F\!\left(-1,\frac32,\frac{13}{2}\right)= \left(-\frac14,0,0\right). \] An invertible function must be injective. Therefore this one collision defeats the conjectured global inverse.

12.4 Why higher dimensions follow

For \(n>3\), append unchanged coordinates: \[ F_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n)= \bigl(F(x_1,x_2,x_3),x_4,\ldots,x_n\bigr). \] The Jacobian matrix is block diagonal, so \[ \det J_{F_n}=-2, \] and the same collision persists. This is the safe, immediate route to all \(n\ge3\).

Certificate-first epistemics The discovery may involve moduli spaces, compactification, ramification, invariant audits, and computer-assisted search. The finished disproof does not inherit that complexity. It compresses to a determinant identity and a witness collision.

13.1 What remains true if the skeleton fails

The explicit polynomial map remains a counterexample even if the proposed identification \(X\cong\mathbb A^3\) is later rejected, modified, or replaced. The geometric skeleton is explanatory mathematics, not a logical dependency of the finite certificate.

13.2 The precise loophole

Constant Jacobian eliminates finite ramification. It does not guarantee properness. The missing sheet is not hiding at a finite critical point; it escapes through the boundary of an affine compactification.

→
Global picture
Nonproper escape, changing finite fiber count, collision elsewhere.

13.3 Why the plane may resist

No single slogan is yet a theorem of explanation. Plausible structural differences include the stronger classification theory of affine surfaces, less room for boundary configurations, and tighter restrictions on polynomial maps of the plane. The correct statement is simply that the explicit three-dimensional disproof does not settle dimension \(2\).

Final mental model Roots \(\to\) coefficients \(\to\) mark one root \(\to\) three sheets \(\to\) repeated roots \(\to\) ramification \(\to\) delete finite folds \(\to\) étale map \(\to\) delete a hyperplane \(\to\) affine target \(\to\) recognize the source \(\to\) the one glyph.
Part VI · Things to steal

14 · General problem-solving principles

14.1 Difficulty relocation

Reframing does not destroy hardness; it moves hardness into a different category. Here, the universal problem “understand every constant-Jacobian map” becomes the concrete problem “recognize one particular open threefold.” The new problem may still be hard, but it is existential, explicit, and attackable with coordinates.

Historical parallel Shor’s algorithm relocates factoring into period finding, where the quantum Fourier transform is an available weapon.

Bridge — heuristic In a spectral approach to primes, name exactly where the arithmetic difficulty lands: constructing an operator, proving self-adjointness, controlling the spectrum, or decoding eigenvalues. “Spectral” is useful only when the relocated obstruction is explicit.

14.2 Inherit the pathology; fight for the ambient space

The three-to-one ambiguity is tautological on the marked-root space. The construction does not laboriously manufacture a collision. It finds a moduli space where collision is built in, then spends effort standardizing the ambient spaces.

14.3 Fine-tuned hypotheses map the mechanism

“Tangent but not osculating” is a fingerprint. It produces the intersection divisor \(2p+q\), one exceptional fiber, and a favorable irreducible boundary configuration. Strange hypotheses should be reverse-engineered first; they often tell you where the machine is hidden.

14.4 Invariant matching as adversarial procedure

  1. Enumerate all computable invariants.
  2. Try to disprove the claimed equivalence with each.
  3. Vary every suspicious assumption.
  4. Watch which invariant fails when the assumption is removed.
  5. When all tests pass, identify the remaining unproved glyph.
  6. Demand a certificate at that glyph.

14.5 Certificate-first epistemics

Existence and counterexample claims can compress to finite witnesses. Universal negative claims usually cannot. When directing automated mathematics toward hard problems, prefer success states whose verification cost is tiny relative to their discovery cost.

14.6 Infinity is part of the object

Poles, projective boundaries, ends of manifolds, and noncompact escape are not bookkeeping nuisances. They often carry the obstruction that finite local calculations cannot see. The Jacobian story is a particularly vivid instance: every finite derivative behaves perfectly, while infinity breaks global invertibility.

Glossary

Affine space. Coordinate space with polynomial functions, \(\mathbb A^n=\operatorname{Spec}\mathbb C[x_1,\ldots,x_n]\).
Projective space. Lines through the origin in \(\mathbb C^{n+1}\), recorded by homogeneous coordinates.
Binary form. A homogeneous polynomial in two variables.
Symmetric product. The quotient \(X^n/S_n\), parameterizing unordered tuples with multiplicity.
Effective divisor. A formal nonnegative integer combination of points or codimension-one subvarieties.
Twisted cubic. The degree-\(3\) Veronese image of \(\mathbb P^1\) in \(\mathbb P^3\).
Small diagonal. The locus \(\{p+\cdots+p\}\) in a symmetric product.
Tangent developable. The union of tangent lines to a curve.
Fiber. The preimage \(f^{-1}(y)\) of one target point.
Generic degree. The number of points in a typical finite fiber.
Ramification. The source locus where a finite map fails to be locally unbranched.
Branch locus. The image downstairs of the ramification locus.
Discriminant. A parameter-space equation detecting degenerate members of a family.
Étale. Algebraically locally isomorphic; in the smooth equal-dimensional case, differential invertible everywhere.
Proper. A map that does not allow points to escape to infinity over bounded target sets.
Finite map. An algebraic map whose coordinate ring extension is finite; geometrically, a proper map with finite fibers.
Unit. An invertible element of a ring of regular functions.
Picard group. Isomorphism classes of line bundles, or divisor classes under suitable hypotheses.
\(\chi_c\). Euler characteristic with compact support, additive under algebraic cut-and-paste.
Simply connected at infinity. Loops sufficiently far from a compact core can contract without returning through the core.
Locally nilpotent derivation. A derivation whose sufficiently high iterate kills each ring element; it encodes a \(\mathbb G_a\)-action.
Makar-Limanov invariant. The intersection of kernels of all locally nilpotent derivations.
Cancellation. Recovering \(X\) from \(X\times\mathbb A^1\).
Birational. Isomorphic only on dense open subsets; much weaker than globally isomorphic.
