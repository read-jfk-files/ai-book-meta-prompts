BEFORE YOU WRITE — DO THIS THINKING FIRST
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
1. RESEARCH FOR ACCURACY. If the topic benefits (nearly all do), search/survey the literature to find: the single best organizing THESIS/SPINE; the genuine key theorems/results with correct names, dates, attributions, and exact statements; canonical examples and counterexamples; standard terminology and notation; and authoritative books/surveys for the reading list. EMBED the verified specifics (statements, constants, dates, the exact form of key formulas, canonical counterexamples) directly into the prompt you produce, so the downstream model cannot drift or hallucinate. Prefer precise embedded facts over "the model will know it."

2. FIND THE SPINE AND THE CRUX. Distill ONE organizing thesis — a single idea the whole guide returns to — and make it the narrative spine. Then locate the topic's CRUX: the single load-bearing step, assumption, definition, symmetry-break, or obstruction that everything hinges on (e.g. "where irreversibility enters", "where the theorem's hypotheses fail", "the one line that closes the hierarchy"). Design a SIGNATURE high-visibility callout box that fires exactly at that crux and nowhere misleadingly.

3. DECIDE THE HONESTY SCAFFOLDING. Check whether the topic carries (a) a COMMON MISCONCEPTION that must be corrected up front and reinforced; (b) SPECULATIVE or open material that must be demarcated from established results; (c) ANALOGIES that must be labeled as conceptual kinship vs. rigorous mathematical equivalence. If any apply, add a dedicated CRITICAL INTELLECTUAL-HONESTY REQUIREMENT block and matching callout types, and make getting this right a stated non-negotiable. If none apply, omit that block.

4. DESIGN 2–4 INTERACTIVE WIDGETS (vanilla JS, no libraries). The PRIMARY widget must make the central thesis viscerally clear (ideally by letting the reader manipulate the crux). Add a SECONDARY and, if warranted, a THIRD/OPTIONAL. Specify each concretely enough to build.

5. CHOOSE A TOPIC-SPECIFIC MOTIF and the family of typed callout boxes (including any honesty-specific and the signature-crux box).
